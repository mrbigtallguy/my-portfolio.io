Sea Eagle Autonomous Beach Patrol

Interaction Design Studio (2016)

The Brief

Designing and prototype screen-based interfaces that would enable interactions with autonomous vehicles in future cities.


The Concept

An autonomous aerial surf patrol drone and accompanying tablet application which is designed to improve coordination and efficiency in lifeguard operations, improving safety for people in the water.


USER RESEARCH

We used three methods to gather insights from our users:

Online Survey
Posted on the Sydney subreddit, yielding over 150 responses.

Semi-structured Interviews
5 students between 19 and 22 years of age from the University of Sydney.

Contextual Observations
Observing user interactions to uncover latent needs and issues.

IDEATION

Using think aloud sessions discussing user thoughts on current VR UI findings, we gathered finding from our various users to reveal insights of expectation people have when interacting with VR.

Our users needs were:
Feedback, Harmony and Haptic Connection.

The next step was to settle on an engaging and intuitive design which we could then use to ensure a prototype was made that met our newfound standards. A walking simulation best suited our user needs, as it was a simple simulation that allowed us to focus on a strong sense of immersion.

USER TESTING

Prototyping the chosen UI solution was completed using Unity and Adobe CC. Unity allowed cross platform integration of our simulation which allowed us to give users accessibility to our initial iterations. this also made clear the distinction of standards when comparing the simulation across platforms. The controls were designed to allow the user to control the simulation completely through their gaze. This was reported effective by the users as the effortlessness communicated reminded some user of a leisurely walk. The simplicity of controls also allowed them to focus on the sensory experience which was a combination of vivid vista and spoken word poetry played as the user collected poem fragments
